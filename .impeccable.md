# Impeccable — Pride Guide

Persistent design context for AI-assisted work on this repo. Updated from explicit product direction (March 2026).

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary:** Adults in their 20s–40s in the U.S. queer community—people comfortable with self-expression and identity-forward experiences.

**Also served:** Allies and learners using the app educationally (flags, quiz, ally tips, PRISM context).

**Context:** Casual web sessions; mobile-friendly patterns (tabs, search, scroll-from-hero). **Job to be done:** Explore flags with accurate history and meaning, build knowledge, feel represented and energized—without leaning on community members for unpaid education.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** *Celebration. Non-conformity. Individuality.*

**Voice:** Grassroots, justice-oriented (PRISM: representation, inclusivity, social justice). Educational copy stays respectful and clear; the *feel* should be bold and alive—not cautious or corporate.

**Anti-references (avoid):** Conformity, conservative restraint, timid “play it safe” UI, cold or unempathetic tone.

**Emotional goals:** Energy, excitement, motivation—an **activist** pulse that gets people fired up while still trusting the information.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **North star:** Push **beyond default SaaS UI**. Prefer **unexpected juxtaposition**: a **hybrid** of (a) **8-bit / retro-game / pixel-read** presentation—especially the column-offset “pixel wave” language already used in flag animations (chunky vertical slices, stepped edges, saturated flat color blocks)—and (b) something **hyper-modern** (sharp type, glow, motion, contemporary layout). The mix is metaphorical: visibility, refusal of a single box, pride as both heritage and future.
- **Not full retro:** Do not land entirely in nostalgia UI; the contrast between retro-digital texture and modern structure is the point.
- **Stack (baseline):** Next.js App Router, Tailwind, shadcn/ui (Radix), Lucide, Framer Motion; Inter as primary sans—use these as **substrate**, not a ceiling.
- **Hero / flags:** Black canvas, large gradient-masked type, aurora flags; preserve **accurate flag colors** and respectful representation while exploring more experimental framing.
- **Themes:** Light, dark, and **Chillwave** share the `hsl` token model; experimental treatments should still resolve sensibly in each theme.

### Accessibility & motion

**Goal:** **Maximize accessibility** while people **without** relevant impairments get an **uncompromised** expressive experience.

**Implication:** **Progressive enhancement**—core tasks (read, navigate, search, complete quiz) must work with plain layouts, sufficient contrast, keyboard paths, and screen-reader-friendly structure. Decorative intensity (animation, pixel effects, glow) layers on top but **must** honor **`prefers-reduced-motion`** (and similar preferences) with calmer or static alternatives—not a degraded product, a **different intentional mode**. Continue patterns like **pause motion on hover** where they help; extend with explicit reduced-motion variants where needed.

### Design Principles

1. **Symbols stay sacred** — Flag colors, patterns, and narratives stay accurate and community-respecting; experimentation is *presentation*, not distortion of meaning.
2. **Juxtaposition on purpose** — Pair retro/pixel-read surfaces with hyper-modern typography, motion, or layout only when it reinforces non-conformity and energy—not random clutter.
3. **Substance + voltage** — Education and clarity are non-negotiable; energy and activism amplify the message, they don’t replace it.
4. **Accessible by default, expressive by choice** — Ship full-fidelity visuals for users who want them; provide **equal-class** alternatives (not afterthoughts) for reduced motion, contrast needs, and assistive tech.
5. **Tokens across themes** — New visuals hook into CSS variables so light, dark, and Chillwave remain coherent.
6. **shadcn as chassis** — Use Radix primitives and existing components for behavior and a11y baselines; **skin and surround** them experimentally rather than reinventing accessible patterns from scratch.

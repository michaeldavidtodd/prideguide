# Impeccable — Pride Guide

Persistent design context for AI-assisted work on this repo. **Inferred sections** below come from the codebase and on-screen copy; refine after you answer the open questions in chat.

## Design Context

### Users

**Inferred:** People exploring LGBTQIA+ flags and symbols—community members seeking representation and history, allies building understanding, and curious learners. **Context:** Short sessions on the web (mobile-friendly tabs, search, scroll-from-hero pattern). **Job to be done:** Quickly find a flag, understand meaning and history, test knowledge via quiz, or read ally guidance—without asking marginalized people to do unpaid education.

### Brand Personality

**Inferred from PRISM / About copy and UI:** Grassroots, multimedia-forward, justice-oriented. **Three words:** *celebratory, educational, inclusive.* **Emotional goals:** Pride and warmth (gradients, sparkles, flag motion), clarity and trust for facts (card layout, structured tabs), and a sense of belonging—not clinical detachment.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Stack:** Next.js App Router, Tailwind, shadcn/ui (Radix), Lucide, Framer Motion; Inter as the primary sans.
- **Hero:** Full-viewport black canvas, large uppercase “PRIDE / GUIDE” with animated flag gradients clipped to text, aurora-style animated flags behind, “BY PRISM” subtitle, primary CTA as a simple white pill button.
- **Main content:** Rounded top sheet over the hero (`main-content`) with soft pastel gradient (pink → purple → blue) in light mode; slate gradient in dark; **Chillwave** theme adds deep purple base, pink/orange/yellow accents, neon-style glows on cards and hero text.
- **Flag visuals:** Real flag hex values and named gradients in CSS (e.g. `pride-flag-gradient`, `trans-flag-gradient`); animated columns with hover-to-pause for motion sensitivity.
- **UI chrome:** Purple–pink gradient title treatments, `muted-foreground` for secondary text, badges for categories, 4-column tab bar for Flags / Quiz / Ally / About.

### Design Principles

1. **Accuracy and respect for symbols** — Flag colors, patterns, and copy should reflect real community usage; avoid trivializing or flattening identity into decoration only.
2. **Education first, spectacle second** — Motion and gradients draw people in; hierarchy, search, filters, and readable cards deliver the lesson.
3. **Motion is optional** — Prefer patterns that allow reduced interruption (e.g. pause on hover); consider `prefers-reduced-motion` for future work.
4. **Three-theme system** — Light, dark, and Chillwave share one token model (`hsl` CSS variables); new surfaces should respect all three.
5. **shadcn consistency** — Extend existing Card, Tabs, Button, Input patterns; keep radius and spacing aligned with `--radius` and container padding.
